How It Works
From raw footage to live posts across 25 accounts, every single day
The Core Process

Every video goes through a 4-stage quality process before it reaches any account. Nothing goes live without being reviewed by a human. The process is built this way deliberately: volume at this scale only works if every step has a clear owner and a clear standard.

1
Patrick's team uploads raw content to the shared Google Drive folder
Our clip coordinators are notified immediately and begin reviewing within the hour.
2
Clip coordinators watch the full footage and identify the best moments
They flag exact timestamps with notes on why each moment has viral potential. Finding the right 30 seconds in a 30-minute video is where most value is created.
3
Editors receive the brief and cut the clips within 4 hours
Hook in 3 seconds, clean audio, styled captions, vertical format. 8 editors working in parallel means multiple videos are in production simultaneously.
4
Copywriters write 5 captions per video, one per platform
YouTube SEO, Instagram hook, TikTok short-form, Facebook conversational, Snapchat one-liner. Nothing is copy-pasted across platforms.
5
Content reviewers watch every finished clip and approve or send back for revisions
Status: Approved or Changes Required with specific written notes. Nothing goes live without this sign-off.
6
Approved content is posted across all 25 accounts in 5 batches throughout the day
Each account posts at its optimal time. No two accounts post simultaneously. All posting is managed and monitored by our team.
7
Performance is tracked at 1 hour and 24 hours after each post
Our reporting specialist updates the tracker with views, saves, shares, and comments from every platform. High performers are flagged immediately to the whole team.
Steps 1-2
Content Discovery: The Most Important Step
Clip Coordinators
1
Clip coordinators watch Patrick's raw footage in full
Not skim. Full watch. They are looking for specific 20-40 second windows with genuine emotional or informational hooks. This is where viral potential is identified before editing begins.
2
They flag timestamps with specific briefing notes for editors
Example: "0:14:32 to 0:15:08 Patrick drops a contrarian statement about failure. Strong hook potential. Open with the statement, not the setup."
3
Three clips are selected per day with variety in mind
Different topics, different energy levels, different hooks to maximise the range of content going live each day and find the formats that resonate most.

Our Intelligence Layer
8 intelligence systems running behind the scenes so the human team always makes the right call
What Separates Us From Every Other Agency

Every agency has editors. Every agency has social media managers. What most agencies do not have is an intelligence layer that watches every post, analyses what is working across the entire niche, spots trends before they peak, and gives the team data-backed direction every single week. We built that layer ourselves. It runs continuously so the human team never has to guess.

The 8 Intelligence Systems

System 01
Content Intelligence
Runs daily, every post
Analyses every post across all 25 accounts within 1 hour and 24 hours of publishing. Tracks views, saves, shares, and comments. Identifies which content formats are outperforming and feeds that data directly to the clip coordinators and copywriters so the team can act on it immediately.
System 02
Trend Research
Every Sunday
Every Sunday, our research system scans the top performing accounts in the motivational and personal brand niche across TikTok and Instagram. It identifies trending formats, rising topics, and the specific hooks generating the most views that week. The team starts every Monday with this brief in hand.
System 03
Weekly Strategy
Every Monday
Every Monday morning, a full week-in-review is compiled from all performance data. It identifies what worked, what underperformed, and what to test in the coming week. This brief shapes the entire week's content selection and posting strategy for the human team.
System 04
Performance Reporting
Every 3 days
Every 3 days, a clean performance snapshot is compiled across all platforms and all accounts. Total posts, total views, platform breakdown, top performing content, and goal progress. Goes directly to David so he always has the most current picture without waiting for the weekly report.
System 05
Caption Optimisation
Every video
Our caption system analyses the content of each video and generates 5 platform-specific caption drafts which our copywriters then review and refine. This ensures every caption is built around the specific moments in the video rather than generic descriptions, and adapts to each platform's algorithm.
System 06
Competitor Intelligence
Weekly
Every week, our system monitors what the top accounts in Patrick's niche are doing. Which formats are gaining traction, which topics are rising, what hooks are generating saves. This keeps Patrick's content distribution ahead of the curve rather than reacting to it.
System 07
Audience Research
Weekly
Our audience research system mines YouTube comments, Reddit threads, and TikTok comment sections in the motivational niche to understand what the audience is actively asking for, saving, and sharing. This shapes what clips our coordinators prioritise and what topics the copywriters emphasise.
System 08
Goal and Viral Tracking
Every Friday
Every Friday, the entire system's data is reviewed against the 25 million views target and the viral milestone goals. It calculates whether the campaign is on pace, ahead, or behind, identifies which posts are approaching viral thresholds, and outputs a single strategic recommendation for the following week.
How Intelligence and Human Work Together

The intelligence systems do not post content, manage accounts, or make creative decisions. They process data and surface insights. Clip coordinators use those insights to find better moments. Editors use them to refine cuts. Copywriters use them to write better captions. Social media managers use them to engage with the right audience at the right time. The intelligence makes the humans more effective. The humans make every decision.

The Goal: Viral Clips and Real Audience Growth
Volume builds momentum. Momentum creates viral clips. Viral clips build real audiences.
What We Are Actually Building

The 25 million views target is the floor, not the ceiling. The real goal is different from what most agencies talk about. We are not trying to accumulate 25 million views across thousands of small posts. We are using high-volume posting to find the 10, 20, or 30 clips that have the potential to hit 1 million, 5 million, and 10 million views on their own. Those are the clips that change everything for Patrick's Instagram. One 10-million-view clip brings more real followers to Patrick's main account than a thousand ordinary posts ever will.

Why Volume Creates Viral

Most content creators post once or twice a day and hope something lands. We post 72 times per day across 25 accounts. That volume is not just about accumulating views. It is the mechanism that finds the moments with viral potential. Every clip is a test. The algorithm tells us within 24 hours which ones have the signal. When a clip tracks above average, our team loads more of that format immediately. This is how you find the clips that go from 50,000 views to 5 million.
