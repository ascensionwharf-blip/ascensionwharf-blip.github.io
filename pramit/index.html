5
The AI Pipeline
Phase 5, everything from approval to analytics, zero human input
Trigger

This entire phase starts the instant a Google Sheet row changes to "Approved". The AI handles captions, vertical conversion, batch posting across all 25 accounts, analytics, flagging, and weekly reporting automatically.

Step 1
Caption Generation via Claude API (30 seconds)

Claude Sonnet 4.6 analyses the video content and writes 5 distinct captions, one per platform. Every caption is specific to what is actually in the video. Never a template.

PlatformCaption Format
YouTube ShortsSEO title under 60 chars with #Shorts, 3-4 sentence description, 8 tags
Instagram ReelsHook under 125 chars, 2-3 punchy lines, 10 hashtags in first comment only
FacebookConversational 3-5 sentences with 3-5 inline hashtags
TikTok1-2 lines max, ultra-short, 3-5 trending hashtags, under 150 chars total
Snapchat SpotlightOne line only, under 80 chars, no hashtags, reads like a text message
Step 2
Vertical Conversion (20 seconds on server)

The server converts the edited video from its original format to 1080x1920 (9:16 vertical) with a blurred background. Required by all short-form platforms. The original file is kept separately and never overwritten.

Step 3
Batch Posting via Zernio Across All 25 Accounts

n8n schedules Zernio API calls in 5 batches spread throughout the day. No two accounts post at the exact same time. This prevents spam detection and maximises algorithmic reach. Full batch schedule is in Section 6.

Step 4
Analytics at 1 Hour and 24 Hours

n8n fires automatically 1 hour after each post and again at 24 hours. Zernio pulls views, saves, shares, and comments from all platforms in one API call. Google Sheet is updated automatically. No separate YouTube, Instagram, or Facebook API calls needed.

If any video's 24-hour views are 2x or more above the week's average, n8n writes a HIGH PERFORMER flag to the Leaderboard tab and sends a Slack alert to #high-performers immediately.

Batch Posting Schedule
5 batches, staggered throughout the day, no simultaneous posts
Why Batches

With 5 accounts per platform each posting 3 videos per day, posting all at the same time would look like coordinated spam to platform algorithms. Instead, each batch posts at a different time window. Each batch contains one account from every platform. Batch 1 is always the main accounts. Batches 2 through 5 are the secondary account sets.

Spacing Note

On Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat, the batches are spaced 15 to 20 minutes apart. On YouTube and Facebook, batches are 20 minutes apart. This prevents any platform from seeing multiple posts from related accounts at the same timestamp, which reduces the risk of algorithmic suppression or spam flags.

Every agent has exactly one job. Agents do not overlap. When a video is approved, the agents fire in sequence: Caption, then Posting, then Analysis. Every Monday, Strategy fires. Every Sunday, Research fires. Every 3 days, Reporter fires. The CEO Agent reviews the full picture weekly and flags anything that needs a strategic decision.

Setup Timeline
4-week plan from zero accounts to full live operation
Total Time: 3 to 4 Weeks

Most of the time is account warmup. Social platforms will flag and ban new accounts that immediately post at high volume. Accounts need 2 to 3 weeks of normal organic activity before automation begins. The technical setup takes 1 to 2 days.

1
Week 1
Create and begin warming all 25 accounts
Create the 20 secondary accounts (4 per platform) plus the new Snapchat main account. Post 2 to 3 pieces of organic content manually on each account. Let them age. Connect Patrick's existing main accounts on YouTube, TikTok, and Facebook to the warmup schedule. Do not connect automation yet.
2
Week 2
Full technical setup: Zernio, n8n, Claude API, Google, Slack
Sign up for Zernio and connect all 25 accounts (1 to 2 hours). Get Anthropic API key (10 minutes). Set up n8n on a VPS or use cloud version (2 to 3 hours). Create Google Drive folder structure. Set up Google Sheets Tracker with all columns. Create 8 company Gmail accounts. Set up all Slack channels. Brief the team on roles. This entire technical setup takes approximately one full working day.
3
Week 3
Build and test all n8n workflows and Reminder Agent rules
Build all n8n workflows for the 8 agents, the batch posting schedule, and all 5 Reminder Agent rules. Test each one end-to-end with 3 real videos. Verify every Slack message fires correctly. Verify Zernio posts land on the right accounts at the right times. Check all 5 batch time slots are working. Fix any timing or integration issues before going live.
4
Week 4 onwards
Go live. Full operation begins. Monitor closely for 3 days.
Accounts are warmed. Workflows are tested. Team is briefed. Day 1 of full live operation: 3 videos uploaded, pipeline runs end-to-end, 72 posts go out in 5 batches across the day. Monitor the first 3 days closely. Check that no accounts get flagged. Verify analytics are returning from Zernio. Confirm the Reminder Agent is firing correctly. After Day 3 with no issues, the system runs on its own.

Every missed target is caught by the CEO Agent before it becomes unfixable. Patrick and Vedant always know exactly what is happening. They just do not need to manually manage it.
